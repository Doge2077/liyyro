## 2\. Alexander Nevsky Cathedral, Sofia

---

**[St. Alexander Nevsky](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Alexander_Nevsky&gt;) Cathedral** is a [Bulgarian Orthodox](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarian_Orthodox&gt;) cathedral in [Sofia](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sofia&gt;), the capital of [Bulgaria](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria&gt;). Built in [Neo-Byzantine style](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-Byzantine_style&gt;), it serves as the [cathedral](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathedral&gt;) church of the [Patriarch of Bulgaria](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patriarch_of_Bulgaria&gt;) and it is believed to be one of [the 50 largest Christian church buildings](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_church_buildings&gt;) by volume in the world. It is one of Sofia's symbols and primary tourist attractions.St. Alexander Nevsky Cathedral in Sofia occupies an area of 3,170 square metres (34,100 sq ft) and can hold 5,000 people inside.It is believed to be among the 10 [largest Eastern Orthodox church buildings](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_Eastern_Orthodox_church_buildings&gt;). It is the largest cathedral in the [Balkans](&lt;https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans&gt;). It is believed that until year 2000 it was the largest finished Orthodox cathedral.

## 3\. Seven Rila Lakes

---

The **Seven Rila Lakes** are a group of [glacial lakes](&lt;https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glacial_lake&gt;), situated in the northwestern [Rila Mountain](&lt;https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rila_Mountain&gt;) in [Bulgaria](&lt;https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria&gt;). They are the most visited group of lakes in [Bulgaria](&lt;https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria&gt;). The lakes are situated between 2,100 and 2,500 metres elevation above sea level.The Seven Lakes are a tourist attraction in Bulgaria because of its natural environment.
